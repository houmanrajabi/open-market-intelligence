\chapter*{Abstract}
Regulatory compliance professionals face significant challenges in monitoring evolving policy documents due to information overload. While Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) systems offer a potential solution, their reliability is compromised by "silent failures" (hallucinations) and the high cost of human oversight. This thesis investigates a "Self-Monitoring" architecture that combines token-level entropy estimation with Reinforcement Learning from AI Feedback (RLAIF) to improve citation accuracy and grounding. Using the FOMC Meeting Minutes (2015-2024) as a case study, we develop a hybrid visual-textual extraction pipeline and evaluate the system's ability to signal uncertainty when information is insufficient. The results aim to provide a reproducible framework for safer document-grounded generation in high-stakes domains.
